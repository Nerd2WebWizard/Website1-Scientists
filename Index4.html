Who Was Benjamin Franklin?

In addition to being a writer, scientist, inventor, statesman, diplomat, printer, publisher, forger, and political philosopher, Benjamin Franklin was also an American polymath. Franklin was the tenth of seventeen children born to a father who produced soap and candles, one of the most menial of artisan professions. In an era when the firstborn son was favoured, Franklin was, as he wryly remarked in his Autobiography, "the youngest Son of the youngest Son for five Generations back."He learnt to read at a young age and spent one year in grammar school and another with a personal instructor, but his official education stopped at the age of ten. At the age of 12, he became an apprentice to his printer brother James. His mastery of the printer's art, which he was proud of till the end of his life, was accomplished between 1718 and 1723. During this time, he frequently read and taught himself.
